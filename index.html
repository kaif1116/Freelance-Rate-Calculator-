The calculator uses your monthly income goal, weekly working hours, weeks worked per year, and optional expenses to determine your hourly rate. It then calculates your daily and monthly rates based on that hourly rate.

Should I include expenses in my rate calculation? â–¼

Yes, including expenses gives you a more accurate rate that covers your business costs. Expenses can include software subscriptions, equipment, internet, insurance, taxes, and any other costs related to your freelance business.

How many weeks per year should I work as a freelancer? â–¼

Most freelancers work between 45-48 weeks per year, accounting for holidays, sick days, and vacation time. Adjust this based on your personal needs and the nature of your freelance work.

Are these rates suitable for beginners? â–¼

The calculator provides rates based on your income goals. Beginners might need to adjust their rates based on market conditions and experience level. Consider starting slightly lower and increasing rates as you gain experience and build a portfolio.

How do I adjust for different project types? â–¼

For fixed-price projects, estimate the total hours and multiply by your hourly rate. For value-based pricing, consider the project's value to the client rather than just the time spent. The calculator gives you a baseline to work from.
