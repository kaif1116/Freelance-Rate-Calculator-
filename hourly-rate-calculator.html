Frequently Asked Questions

1. How many billable hours should I realistically expect per week?

For most freelancers, 25-30 billable hours per week is realistic. This accounts for: marketing/sales (5-10 hours), administrative tasks (5-8 hours), professional development (5-7 hours), and breaks/vacation time. If you're consistently hitting 35+ billable hours, you're likely neglecting business growth activities.

2. What expenses should I include in my rate calculation?

Include ALL business expenses: software subscriptions, equipment/computer depreciation, internet/phone, office supplies, professional insurance, accounting/tax services, marketing costs, professional development courses, healthcare (if self-funded), retirement contributions, and estimated taxes (25-30% of income). Our calculator's expense breakdown helps capture these.

3. How much profit margin should I include?

Aim for 20-40% profit margin. Beginners: 20-25% (covers emergencies, slow periods). Experienced: 25-35% (funds growth, equipment upgrades). Experts: 30-40%+ (allows for investments, team hiring). Profit margin = (Revenue - All Expenses) Ã· Revenue Ã— 100. This isn't personal incomeâ€”it's business profit for growth.

4. Should I charge different hourly rates for different types of work?

Yes, consider tiered rates: 1) Core service rate (your standard rate), 2) Premium/urgent rate (25-50% higher for rush work), 3) Consulting/strategy rate (higher for advisory work), 4) Maintenance/support rate (lower retainer rate for ongoing work). Always communicate rate differences clearly to clients.

5. How do I handle rate increases with existing clients?

Give 60-90 days notice. Explain the increase in terms of added value: "I've completed [certification/training] and improved [specific skills], allowing me to deliver even better results." For long-term clients, offer a smaller increase or grandfather them at current rates for a limited time. Always put rate changes in writing.

6. What if my calculated rate is much higher than market rates?

First, verify your calculations are realistic. If correct, consider: 1) Targeting higher-paying clients/industries, 2) Specializing in a niche with less competition, 3) Improving your portfolio/testimonials to justify higher rates, 4) Offering exceptional value that commands premium pricing, 5) Starting with slightly lower rates and increasing as you prove value.

7. How do I track my actual billable hours accurately?

Use time tracking software like Toggl, Harvest, or Clockify. Track ALL time: client work, communication, research, revisions. Review weekly to understand your actual billable vs non-billable ratio. After 3 months, use this data to adjust your rate calculations. Many freelancers discover they're only 50-60% billable.

8. Can I use this calculator for different currencies?

Yes, our calculator works with any currency. Simply enter amounts in your local currency. The country selection helps with market rate comparisons. For currency conversion for international clients, use current exchange rates and consider currency fluctuation risks in your pricing.
